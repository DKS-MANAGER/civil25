\section*{Problem statement}
The probability of rain $X$ on a given day during the monsoon season at a location is $0.4$. The rainfall depth (mm) on rainy days follows a Gamma distribution with density
\[
f_X(x)=\frac{x^{\alpha-1}e^{-x/\beta}}{\Gamma(\alpha)\beta^{\alpha}},\quad x>0,
\]
with parameters $\alpha=5$ and $\beta=3$. The surface runoff (mm) on day $t$ is
\[
sr_t = d\frac{S_{t-1}}{S_{\max}} x_t,
\]
baseflow is
\[
bf_t = c S_{t-1},
\]
and infiltration is
\[
inf_t = a x_t.
\]
Parameters used: $d=0.3$, $c=0.5$ (unless changed for part (e)), $a=0.6$, $S_{\max}=500$ mm, initial storage $S_0=100$ mm. A total of 1000 simulations of 120 days were generated.

\section*{Method}
For each simulation: (1) generate a 120-day rainfall series with daily rain occurrence probability 0.4 and rainy-day amounts sampled from Gamma(5,3); (2) iterate the storage and compute daily surface-runoff, baseflow and infiltration using the given equations; (3) compute daily streamflow $Q_t = sr_t + bf_t$ and record the maximum daily streamflow for the simulation. Repeated for $\beta=3$ (baseline), $\beta=7$, and for $c=0.1$.

\section*{Discussion}
Increasing $\beta$ (scale) from 3 to 7 increases the mean rainy-day rainfall from $\alpha\beta=15$ mm to 35 mm, producing more intense events. This increases variability in maximum streamflow and allows for rarer extreme peaks; in the simulations the std of maxima increased notably (0.49 â†’ 1.28 mm). Reducing the baseflow coefficient $c$ from 0.5 to 0.1 dramatically reduces maximum streamflow because baseflow is proportional to storage and contributes substantially to the total streamflow in this model.
